# Gameplay Design Rules

## Tone
- Relaxed survival. The player should feel challenged but not punished.
- Death or severe setbacks should be avoidable through reasonable play.
- Do not introduce mechanics that feel oppressive, especially in early game.
- The atmosphere is calm exploration with growing stakes — not anxiety-driven.

## Early Game
- The player starts with nothing on a small dock surrounded by ocean.
- The Hook is the first meaningful tool — reaching nearby islands to gather resources.
- Early game must be legible: the player should understand what to do without a tutorial.
- Do not gate critical early resources (wood, stone, food) behind mid-tier crafting.
- First 5–10 minutes should not result in failure under normal play.

## Progression
- Exploration drives progression. New islands offer new resources and biomes.
- Each tier of crafting should unlock new capabilities, not just stat increases.
- Renewable systems (farming, fishing nets) should require setup effort before they pay off.
- Progression should feel earned, not arbitrary.

## Survival Stats
- Hunger drains at 0.1/s. Thirst drains at 0.2/s. Stamina regenerates at 8/s.
- Stats must remain meaningful — they should require active management.
- Stats must not be so punishing that normal exploration triggers a death spiral.
- Critical warnings trigger at 15% — UI must make this visible.
- Cooked food should provide meaningfully better restoration than raw food.

## Weather
- Rain: waters crops, fills barrels. Atmospheric but also useful.
- Fog: reduces visibility. Atmospheric, mild penalty.
- Thunderstorm: dramatic. Should have at least one gameplay consequence beyond visuals.
- Weather should support the atmosphere without becoming a primary threat on its own.

## Renewable Systems
- Farming: requires planter, water, time. Sustainable mid-game food.
- Fishing (rod): active effort, moderate reward.
- Catching net: passive, low yield, requires setup.
- Resource nodes (trees, rocks): respawn over time. Players should not be able to permanently exhaust an island.

## Exploration
- The ocean world has ~80 islands. Players should be rewarded for sailing to new ones.
- Different biomes should offer distinct resources or visual identity.
- The map system exists to support navigation — it should reveal as the player explores.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Mechanics that punish the player for experimenting.
- Systems that require external knowledge to understand.
- Renewable food locked behind late-game progression.
- Any single resource becoming a permanent bottleneck.
- Weather events that are purely punishing with no upside.
